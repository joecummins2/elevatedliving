About the Role

We are looking for a compassionate and reliable part-time Support Worker to join our team in providing excellent care to children and young people in our residential setting. This is an ideal opportunity for someone looking to gain experience in children's care or seeking flexible working arrangements.

As a Support Worker, you'll work alongside our experienced care team to ensure the children in our care feel safe, supported, and valued. You'll help with daily activities, provide emotional support, and contribute to creating a positive, nurturing environment.

This role offers excellent training and development opportunities, making it perfect for those starting their career in children's care or returning to work after a break.

Key Responsibilities

  • Provide support and assistance to children and young people with daily living activities
  • Help maintain a safe, clean, and welcoming environment
  • Support children with homework, recreational activities, and life skills development
  • Assist with meal preparation and household tasks
  • Observe and report on children's wellbeing and behavior
  • Follow care plans and maintain accurate records as directed
  • Work collaboratively with the care team and other professionals
  • Participate in team meetings and training sessions
  • Support children during difficult times with patience and understanding
  • Promote positive relationships and model appropriate behavior

Essential Requirements

  • Genuine passion for working with children and young people
  • Good communication and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to remain calm under pressure
  • Flexible approach to working hours including weekends
  • Enhanced DBS check (we will support you to obtain this)
  • Reliable and punctual with good attendance record
  • Basic understanding of safeguarding principles
  • Ability to work as part of a team
  • Physical ability to engage in activities with children
  • Commitment to promoting equality and diversity

Desirable Requirements

  • Experience working with children or young people (paid or voluntary)
  • Level 2 qualification in Health & Social Care, Childcare, or related field
  • First Aid certification
  • Driving licence and access to own transport
  • Knowledge of child development
  • Experience in a care, education, or similar environment
  • Additional languages spoken
