About the Role

We are seeking an experienced and dynamic Deputy Manager to join our leadership team at our residential children's home in Stockport. This is an exciting opportunity for a passionate professional to take on a senior role in delivering outstanding care to vulnerable children and young people.

As Deputy Manager, you will work closely with the Registered Manager to ensure the highest standards of care, supporting and developing the team while maintaining compliance with all regulatory requirements. You'll play a key role in creating a positive, therapeutic environment where children can heal, grow, and thrive.

This position offers excellent career progression opportunities and the chance to make a significant impact on both the children in our care and the professional development of our staff team.

Key Responsibilities

  • Support the Registered Manager in the day-to-day management of the residential home
  • Lead and supervise a team of residential care workers, providing guidance and support
  • Ensure compliance with Ofsted regulations and company policies and procedures
  • Conduct staff supervision sessions and participate in recruitment and selection processes
  • Manage duty rotas and ensure appropriate staffing levels at all times
  • Oversee the implementation of individual care plans and risk assessments
  • Coordinate with external professionals including social workers, healthcare professionals, and education providers
  • Handle safeguarding concerns and incident management procedures
  • Contribute to the continuous improvement of service quality and outcomes for children
  • Support with budgetary management and resource planning
  • Participate in regulatory inspections and ensure preparation for visits
  • Lead by example in direct care work with children and young people

Essential Requirements

  • Level 4 qualification in Leadership and Management for Residential Childcare (or equivalent)
  • Minimum 3 years experience in residential children's care, with supervisory/management experience
  • Strong understanding of children's safeguarding procedures and child protection processes
  • Knowledge of Ofsted regulations and inspection frameworks
  • Enhanced DBS check (current or willingness to obtain)
  • Excellent leadership, communication, and interpersonal skills
  • Ability to manage staff performance and conduct difficult conversations
  • Strong organizational and time management skills
  • Understanding of trauma-informed care and therapeutic approaches
  • Valid UK driving licence and access to a vehicle
  • Commitment to continuous professional development
  • Flexible approach to working hours including evening and weekend cover as required

Desirable Requirements

  • Level 5 qualification in Leadership and Management or equivalent
  • Experience of regulatory inspections and report writing
  • Knowledge of budgetary management and financial planning
  • Training in specific therapeutic interventions (e.g., PACE, Life Story Work)
  • Experience in staff training and development
  • Understanding of education and health planning for looked after children
  • Previous experience in quality assurance and service improvement
  • Knowledge of multi-agency working and case conference participation
